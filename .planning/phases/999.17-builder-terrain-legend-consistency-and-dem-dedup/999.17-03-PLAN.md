<objective>
Fix 3 (resolver-alignment, per D-06 — as CORRECTED by RESEARCH). The phase brief's premise
("relax the POLISH-02 guard") was wrong. Research proved the POLISH-02 guard does NOT block a
visible hillshade on a terrain DEM, and MapLibre 5.x allows one source to back both
`setTerrain({source})` and a `hillshade` layer.

THE ACTUAL BUG (confirmed live by the planner 2026-05-31):
`BuilderMap.tsx` `applyTerrainConfig` resolves the terrain DEM with a `.find()` (around line
389) that requires THREE clauses:

  layer.dataset_id === currentTerrainConfig.source_dataset_id
    && isTerrainCapableDemLayer(layer)
    && style_config.render_mode === 'terrain'

The third clause (`render_mode === 'terrain'`) drops the mesh when the dataset's DEM layer is
in hillshade mode. The viewer (`viewer/hooks/use-viewer-terrain.ts`) resolves terrain purely by
`terrain_config.source_dataset_id` + raster token, "independent of any layer's render_mode"
(its own comment).

THE FIX: remove the `style_config.render_mode === 'terrain'` clause from the `applyTerrainConfig`
`.find()` in `BuilderMap.tsx`. KEEP the `dataset_id === source_dataset_id` clause AND the
`isTerrainCapableDemLayer(layer)` clause (so we still only attach to real DEM raster/vrt layers,
and only when terrain_config points at that dataset). This lets one DEM dataset drive the 3D mesh
while a hillshade-mode layer on the same DEM adds visible relief.

EXPLICIT CONSTRAINT (D-07 resolution): DO NOT touch `map-sync.ts` or the POLISH-02 guard. It
stays byte-for-byte unchanged. This plan only changes the SELECTION criterion in BuilderMap.

Purpose: close the "can't have mesh + visible hillshade on one DEM" gap by aligning the builder
resolver with the proven viewer resolver.
Output: BuilderMap demLayer lookup resolved by source_dataset_id (render_mode clause removed),
plus an extended unit test.
</objective>
