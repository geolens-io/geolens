# GeoLens License FAQ

GeoLens is licensed under the [Business Source License 1.1](LICENSE).
This FAQ explains what that means in plain English.

---

## Can I deploy GeoLens at my company for free?

**Yes.** You can install and run GeoLens for your organization's internal use at no cost,
including for commercial purposes. "Internal use" means use by your organization's own
employees and contractors for your organization's own benefit.

---

## Can a consultant or systems integrator deploy GeoLens for a client?

**Yes.** A consulting firm or systems integrator can set up and operate GeoLens for a
client's internal use. This is covered by the Additional Use Grant — the client's
organization is the end user, not third-party customers of a hosted service.

---

## Can I deploy GeoLens via AWS Marketplace or DigitalOcean Marketplace?

**Yes.** Purchasing and deploying GeoLens through an official marketplace listing is
explicitly permitted. The marketplace is a supported distribution channel, not a
"hosted or managed service to third parties."

---

## Can I offer GeoLens as a hosted or managed service to paying customers?

**No.** You may not offer GeoLens (or a substantially similar product) as a hosted or
managed service to third parties. That is the only restriction in the license.

If you want to build a business around GeoLens, the supported path is deploying it for
your own internal operations or selling implementation services to help others deploy it
themselves.

---

## When does the license convert to open source?

On **2030-02-17**, GeoLens automatically becomes available under the
[MIT License](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT). At that point, you may use,
modify, and redistribute it under MIT terms without restriction.
